# Trust Cascades

## How Social Vouching Creates Funding Momentum

Trust cascades describe how early contributions from close connections create confidence that attracts broader funding. This mirrors traditional lending: you ask family first, then friends, then acquaintances, then institutions.

---

## The Three-Stage Progression

### Stage 1: Core Network
**Who:** Close friends, family (ðŸŸ¢ LOW RISK scores)

Early contributors with strong social ties provide the initial validation. They contribute small amounts ($10-$50) immediately and stake their social reputation.

---

### Stage 2: Extended Network
**Who:** Friends-of-friends, acquaintances (ðŸŸ¡ MEDIUM RISK scores)

After seeing core network validation, the extended network contributes moderate amounts ($20-$100). They trust the judgment of mutual connections.

{% hint style="info" %}
**Information Cascade**

When Alice (well-connected member) funds $30, others infer: "Alice knows the borrower and trusts them. I'll contribute too."
{% endhint %}

---

### Stage 3: Public Lenders
**Who:** Strangers with zero connections (ðŸ”´ HIGH RISK scores)

Public lenders rely on **proxy signals** to decide:
- What's the loan's support strength? (ðŸŸ¢ STRONG is best)
- Did well-connected people fund early?
- How many lenders know the borrower?

Without strong social validation, public funding rarely happens.

## Why It Works

When someone contributes, they're doing two things:

1. **Providing capital** - Direct financial support
2. **Vouching for you** - Staking their social reputation

{% hint style="success" %}
**Key Principle**

A $10 contribution from a close friend (ðŸŸ¢ LOW RISK) is a **stronger trust signal** than $100 from a stranger (ðŸ”´ HIGH RISK).

## Why Trust Cascades Work

**Peer Monitoring:** Group members monitor each other, reducing moral hazard.

**Social Sanctions:** Defaulting damages relationships with everyone who funded, not just anonymous lenders.

**Information Cascades:** Early endorsements influence subsequent lenders.

**Threshold Effects:** Once ~30-40% funded by connected lenders, public funding accelerates.
